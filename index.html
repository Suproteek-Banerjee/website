Lightweight Lattice-Based Key Encapsulation for IoT
Publication Year: 2025
This manuscript presents a fully elaborated Module-LWE based Key Encapsulation Mechanism (KEM) optimized for resource-constrained Internet-of-Things (IoT) devices. Beyond providing formal IND-CPA and IND-CCA security, it delivers a comprehensive treatment of: (1) algorithmic construction and step-by-step derivation, (2) rationale for parameter selection, (3) side-channel aware C implementations with selectable arithmetic kernels (NTT, schoolbook, Karatsuba), (4) practical evaluation across Cortex-M, ESP32, and Raspberry Pi Zero platforms, and (5) trade-offs between security, latency, memory footprint, and energy. This work serves as a reference guide for implementing provably secure lattice-based KEMs in heterogeneous IoT environments.

Suproteek Banerjee is a technologist, researcher, and entrepreneur driven by a deep commitment to advancing ethical and intelligent systems. His work bridges artificial intelligence, computational systems, and decentralized technologies, with a focus on creating solutions that are trustworthy, transparent, and human-centered.

Currently pursuing an MS/BS in Computer Science at Illinois Institute of Technology, Suproteek’s academic and professional journey spans research in AI trust and safety, machine learning alignment, and blockchain applications. He has founded and led ventures such as One Fourth Finance, an AI-powered fintech platform democratizing access to credit and digital assets, and ShaberiScript, a custom-built programming language designed to enhance engineering efficiency.

His projects explore the intersection of AI behavior alignment, IoT security, and decentralized finance, merging research rigor with real-world scalability. Suproteek’s technical foundation includes expertise across AI/ML frameworks, full-stack development, and distributed infrastructure, alongside a passion for building systems that redefine accessibility and fairness in technology.

Beyond research and code, Suproteek embodies the spirit of continuous innovation — integrating ethics, creativity, and computational precision to shape a safer and more inclusive digital future.
